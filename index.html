Current research

How does a mechanical cue become a disease signal?

I study this question in human mitral valve endothelial and interstitial cells. By combining controlled mechanical stimulation with gene perturbation and molecular assays, I connect force sensing to signaling changes and extracellular-matrix remodeling. My earlier work in vascular redox biology and chronic inflammation provides a broader lens on how cellular stress becomes cardiovascular disease.

01

How do disease-associated flow environments alter valve-cell behavior?

Mechanical cues in mitral valve cells

Human mitral valve endothelial and interstitial cell models make it possible to compare healthy and disease-relevant responses under controlled mechanical conditions.

Models & methods MVECs · MVICs · Flexcell stimulation · cell culture

02

How does mechanosensitive channel activation reshape serotonin signaling?

PIEZO1–serotonin signaling

I examine how PIEZO1, serotonin transport, and HTR2B signaling converge on profibrotic programs associated with mitral regurgitation.

Models & methods siRNA · RT-qPCR · western blotting · ELISA

03

How do oxidative and inflammatory stress change cardiovascular tissues?

Redox biology and inflammation

Prior projects in aortic aneurysm, atherosclerosis, and chronic lung inflammation built my foundation in vascular remodeling, immune-cell dynamics, and translational disease models.

Approaches flow cytometry · immunofluorescence · mouse models · zymography
