About

Research at the interface of mechanics, signaling, and tissue remodeling.

I am a Ph.D. student in Bioengineering at the University of Pennsylvania, advised by Dr. Robert J. Levy. My doctoral research investigates how PIEZO mechanotransduction and serotonin receptor signaling contribute to mitral valve remodeling. I use primary human mitral valve endothelial and interstitial cells, controlled mechanical stimulation, siRNA knockdown, RT-qPCR, western blotting, immunofluorescence, ELISA, and related cell and molecular biology approaches.

My broader training spans cardiovascular biology, redox signaling, inflammation, flow cytometry, mouse models, and translational biomaterials. Before Penn, I completed a B.A. in Biochemistry and Molecular Biology at Boston University, where I studied vascular redox biology and extracellular matrix remodeling.

Research focus

Selected themes

01

Valve mechanobiology

Investigating how pathological mechanical environments activate mechanosensitive pathways in human mitral valve cells and contribute to mitral regurgitation-associated remodeling.

02

Serotonin and endothelial signaling

Studying 5-HT-related gene expression, receptor signaling, and endothelial-to-mesenchymal transition-associated programs in valve-derived cell models.

03

Inflammation and redox biology

Applying flow cytometry, ELISA, immunofluorescence, and mouse models to study chronic inflammation, immune-cell dynamics, and vascular redox dysregulation.
